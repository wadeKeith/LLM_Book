\chapter{Preference Learning and Alignment}
\label{ch:preference-learning-alignment}

\abstract{This chapter explains reward modeling, RLHF, PPO, KL regularization, direct preference optimization, reference-free variants, annotation protocols, safety data, and the gap between preference learning and true alignment.}

\section{Chapter Spine}
Alignment methods optimize models toward a stated preference signal. The hard parts are defining the signal, controlling distribution shift, avoiding reward hacking, and measuring whether the resulting behavior is actually better for the intended users.

\section{Core Sections}
\subsection{Reward Models}
Pairwise rankings, Bradley-Terry losses, calibration, annotator disagreement, and overoptimization.

\subsection{RLHF with PPO}
Policy, reference model, KL penalty, reward shaping, value estimates, and rollout cost \cite{ouyang2022training}.

\subsection{Direct Preference Optimization}
DPO, reference models, implicit rewards, and stability tradeoffs \cite{rafailov2023direct}.

\subsection{Safety and Refusal}
Helpfulness-harmlessness tensions, jailbreak resistance, red-teaming, and refusal overgeneralization.

\section{Planned Exercises}
Train a toy reward model from pairwise preferences and inspect how changing the KL coefficient changes policy behavior.
