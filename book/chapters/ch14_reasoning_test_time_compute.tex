\chapter{Reasoning and Test-Time Compute}
\label{ch:reasoning-test-time-compute}

\abstract{This chapter covers chain-of-thought prompting, self-consistency, process supervision, verifiers, tree search, MCTS-style reasoning, RLVR, GRPO-style training, DeepSeek-R1-style pipelines, and the limits of spending more inference compute.}

\section{Chapter Spine}
Reasoning methods allocate computation across steps, samples, branches, or verifiers. The chapter treats them as algorithms with budgets and failure modes, not as proof that a model has human-like reasoning.

\section{Core Sections}
\subsection{Prompted Reasoning}
Chain-of-thought, self-consistency, decomposition, and when explicit traces help \cite{wei2022chain}.

\subsection{Search and Verification}
Tree-of-thought, MCTS, process reward models, and verifier validity \cite{yao2023tree}.

\subsection{Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards}
RLVR, GRPO-style objectives, reward hacking, and reasoning-boundary evaluation \cite{deepseek2025r1,wen2025rlvr}.

\subsection{Inference-Time Scaling}
Sequential, parallel, and tree-structured compute; budget forcing; and limitations on knowledge-intensive tasks \cite{chen2025reasoning,yang2025testtime}.

\section{Planned Exercises}
Compare single-sample answers, self-consistency, and a simple verifier loop on a small math or code benchmark.
